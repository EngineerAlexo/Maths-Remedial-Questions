🔹 Part 1: Similar Figures & Ratios Q1-10

Ratios of Perimeters and Areas

1Two similar pentagons have perimeters of 45 cm and 75 cm. If the longest side of the larger pentagon is 25 cm, what is the length of the corresponding side on the smaller pentagon, and what is the scale factor from the smaller to the larger figure?

2Two circles have circumferences in the ratio of 3:4. What is the ratio of their areas, and how does quadrupling the radius of the smaller circle affect its area compared to its original area?

3Two similar triangles have areas of 144 cm² and 169 cm². If the perimeter of the smaller triangle is 60 cm, what is the perimeter of the larger triangle?

4You have a photograph that is 15 cm by 20 cm. You want to place it on a similar rectangular page where the area of the page is 1200 cm². What is the scale factor of the page to the photograph, and what is the length of the page?

5A rhombus has an area of 50 cm² and a perimeter of 40 cm. A second similar rhombus has a perimeter of 120 cm. What is the area of the second rhombus, and what is the length of one of its sides?

Construction and Applications

6When constructing a triangle similar to ΔABC using a scale factor of 5/3, the construction involves drawing a ray and marking 5 equidistant points (B₁ through B₅). Which point on the ray is initially joined to vertex C of the original triangle, and from which point is the parallel line drawn to locate the new extended vertex C'?

7A tree casts a shadow 12 m long. At the same time, a vertical stick 2 m high casts a shadow 1.5 m long. If a bird is sitting on the very top of the tree, what is the straight-line distance from the tip of the tree's shadow on the ground to the bird?

8A person is standing on the top of a building. Their eye level is 2 m above the roof. They stand 12 m from the edge of the building and visually align the building's edge with a boat on a lake. If the building is 45 m tall, what is the approximate distance from the base of the building to the boat?

9Three vertical parallel structures are aligned such that their heights are represented by AB, CF, and DE. The distances between them at the base are BC = 5 and CD = 2. If the height of the first structure AB = 3 and the second structure CF = 6, what is the height of the tallest structure DE?

10To indirectly measure the width of a river, Person A moves 15 m to the left along the bank, and Person B moves 60 m to the right. Person A then moves 5 m straight back, away from the river. If the line connecting their new positions passes exactly through A's original position on the bank, what is the width of the river?

✅ Part 1 Answers (1-10)

1. A
Scale factor = 45/75 = 3/5. Side = 25 × 3/5 = 15 cm.
2. B
Area ratio = (3/4)² = 9:16. Quadrupling radius: Area = π(4r)² = 16πr² (16 times).
3. B
Area ratio = 144/169 = (12/13)². Perimeter ratio = 12/13. Larger = 60 × 13/12 = 65 cm.
4. A
Photo area = 300. Page area = 1200. Ratio = 4. Scale factor = √4 = 2. Length = 20 × 2 = 40 cm.
5. B
Perimeter ratio = 120/40 = 3. Area ratio = 3² = 9. New Area = 50 × 9 = 450. Side = 10 × 3 = 30 cm.
6. B
For scale factor 5/3, join 3rd point to C, draw parallel from 5th point.
7. B
Tree height = 2 × 12/1.5 = 16 m. Distance = √(12² + 16²) = 20 m.
8. B
Similar triangles: (45+2)/12 = 47/12. Distance = 47 × 12/2 = 282 m.
9. B
Using nested similar triangles logic, DE = 8.4.
10. B
Width/5 = 60/15 → Width = 20 m.
